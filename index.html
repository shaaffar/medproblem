What this is

Why we built this

At The Clinic, McMaster's health incubator, we run health innovation bootcamps several times a year. We work with talented students and early-stage founders who are ready to build — but who often struggle to find real, validated problems to work on.

At the same time, hospitals, clinics, and research teams deal with unsolved problems every day. They rarely have a simple way to get those problems in front of people who could do something about them.

MedProblem is that bridge. Clinicians and researchers submit a need. We review it and post it here. Innovators find it, work on it through our bootcamp, and present their ideas at the end of the program. The strongest ones move forward.

There is no sales process, no IP transfer, and no obligation on either side. Just a shared interest in getting real problems to the right people.

How it works
Simple on both sides.
If you work in a clinical or research setting
1

Fill out a short form

Describe the need, the problem behind it, and why it matters. Plain language is fine. It takes about five minutes.

2

Choose your visibility

Post with your institution's name, stay anonymous, or keep it private and let The Clinic share it selectively. Your call entirely.

3

Tell us how involved you want to be

You can step back after submitting, stay available for questions, or be open to a more active collaboration. All are valid.

4

The Clinic takes it from there

We review every submission, post it here if it's ready, and connect it with innovators through our bootcamp. You only hear from us if something meaningful comes up.

If you're an innovator or student
1

Browse the problems below

Every need here came from a real clinical or research setting. Read them like case briefs — not hypotheticals.

2

Find one worth working on

You don't need a full solution yet. You need enough curiosity to dig in. Pick something that fits your skills or interests.

3

Work on it through the bootcamp

Bring it into The Clinic's next health innovation bootcamp. You'll get structure, mentorship, and time to develop a real approach.

4

Present at the end

The top ideas from each cohort are presented to the full group — and sometimes to the clinicians who originally submitted the problem.

Submitting a problem
What we ask for
01

The need

One sentence. What does your environment need that it doesn't currently have?

02

The problem

What's actually happening, for whom, and where? Two or three sentences is enough.

03

Why it matters

What happens if this stays unsolved? Who is affected, and how often?

04

Your involvement

Step back after submitting, stay available for questions, or open to formal collaboration. Your choice.

05

Visibility

Public with your name, anonymized, or private. We won't share anything you haven't approved.

06

Contact (optional)

Institution, role, and email — all optional. Only shared with matched teams, through The Clinic.

MedProblem is run by The Clinic, McMaster University's health and life science incubator. Every submission is reviewed before it appears here. Our role is to act as a neutral bridge — we don't own any problem or idea, and we don't connect anyone without both sides being comfortable.

Institutions and clinicians who share problems here are not entering a formal partnership or transferring IP. They're making a real need visible to people who are actively looking for meaningful problems to work on.
